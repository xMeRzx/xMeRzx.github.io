What makes a good build?

Articulation

Posing is everything, so naturally having good articulation in a figure really improves its quality. Articulation essentially means how many points there are on a character to move and pose. The more articulation points the character has, the better. The number of points, however, can be quite limited when it comes to tinier builds.

Color scheme

Color scheme, or color palette, is important for the aesthetic look of the character. Unusual, consistency breaking or clashing color combinations are usually frowned upon, although complementary colors - that is, opposite colors on the color wheel - are known to work in most cases.

Functions

Many story points or character abilities rely on so called functions or action features. Well executed functions improve the figure's design and also enchance play value. Some well known set functions are, for example, moving legs when rolling a set across a flat surface or a gear system within a character's torso which allows them to swing their arms or legs.
